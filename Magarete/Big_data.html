BIG DATA: Dreaming Big Enough

Share with

Whatsapp Facebook G-plus Twitter 
'Big-data’ is similar to ‘Small-data’, but bigger. It is more than simply a matter of size. Now a day trend is of Big Data. Big data can be think of as server’s log, GB’s of file. Every day Petabytes of data processed by Big MNCS. Big Data — is a pretty common concept in IT and digital marketing. Essentially, the definition is on the surface: the term “big data” implies managing and an analysis of big volumes of data. Broadly speaking, this is information which cannot be processed by classical approaches due to its volume
“Big data is at the foundation of all the megatrends that are happening today, from social to mobile to cloud to gaming.” – Chris Lynch, Vertica Systems
Big Data has certain characteristics and hence is defined using 4Vs namely:
Volume:”Scale of Data”. The amount of data that businesses can collect is really enormous. We used to store data from sources like spreadsheets and databases. Now data comes in the form of emails, photos, videos, monitoring devices, PDFs, audio, etc. 
•	It is estimated that 2.5 Quintillion Bytes (2.3 Trillion GB) of data are created each day.
•	By 2020, 40 ZETABYTES (43 Trillion GB) of data will be created is 300 times increases by the time of 2005.
•	World population: 7 Billion, Out of that 6 Billion Cell phones.

Velocity: It refers to “Data in Motion”. The rate at which new data is being generated due to our dependence on the internet, sensors, and machine-to-machine data is also important to parse Big Data in a timely manner.
•	Examples like By 2016, it is projected there will be 18.9 Billion Network connections- almost 2.5 connections per person on Earth. 
•	Modern car have close to 100 Sensors that monitor items such as fuel level and tyre pressure. 
•	New York Stock Exchange captures 100 TB of trade information during each trading sessions. 

Variety:”Different Form of Data”. The data that is generated is completely heterogeneous in the sense that it could be in various formats like video, text, database, sensor data and so on and hence understanding the type of Big Data is a key factor.
•	330 Billion Pieces of content are shared on Facebook every month.
•	400 Millions Tweets are sent per day by about 200 milion monthly active users.
•	4 billion hours+ hours of video are watched on YouTube every month.

Veracity: “Uncertainty of DATA”. Knowing whether the data that is available is coming from a credible source is of utmost importance before deciphering and implementing Big Data for business needs.
•	1 in 3 Business Leaders don’t trust the information they use to make decisions.
•	Poor data quality costs the US economy around $3.1 Trillion  a year.
•	27% of Respondents in one Survey were unsure of how much of their data was inaccurate.
